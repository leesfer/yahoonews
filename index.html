Loma Linda One of Six Schools Cancelling Speech Therapy Program This Year

By DAVID McHUGH and JOAN LOWY
1 hour ago



LOMA LINDA, California, US (AP) â€” Loma Linda University announced today that they would be one of the six schools on the West Coast which would be cancelling their on-going Speech Therapy and Audiology programs.

"It was not an easy decision to make," says university's dean, Roger Hadley, "this program has been a staple at Loma Linda for many years, but our future needs to move in a different medical direction at this time."

As with the other six schools, Loma Linda University would be closing the program to make room for other medical fields.

The current press releases states that all current students in the program would be required to transfer to a secondary university if they wish to continue studying their program. Lomda Linda staff would be completely available to all students looking to change schools on short notice.

There will be an announcement for all current students going out later today, along with a number of options about how they can move forward from here.

This news comes as a big shock after the announcement of a new medical building earlier this year. The school promised a $1.6 billion addition to the medical campus, however it now looks as if this new building is another piece of Loma Linda's "new medical future", which, unfortunately, doesn't seem to have room for Speech Pathology nor Audiology.

"We may choose to re-introduce these two programs at a later time, but we currently are slimming down our offerings to focus on the more licrative programs," continues Dean Roger Hadley. "We absolutely regret having our current students put into this tough position, but we will be there to give any help necessary to make this move."
